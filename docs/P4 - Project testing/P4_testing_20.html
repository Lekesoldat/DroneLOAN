Testing Document

A brief summary of the questionnaire you send out to testers, as well as the link to the Google Form you used. Don't forget to ask your client to test the website.

We chose to split the testing in two different parts: Google form survey and live testing.

With our Google forms we wanted to gather data on topics such as design, functionality, accessibility, performance and potential changes which could be implemented. In the form we ask questions such as: 'How pleasing is the apperance?' and 'Did ever require more than three clicks to get to your desitination?'. After a sufficent number of surveys is submitted, we aim to analyse the data and make potential changes to our site.

Link to the Google Form

The live testing differs from the form survey, because in the live testing we ask the test subject to perform different tasks on the website. We aim to see if the design, accessibility and functionality combined make sense to a person who never have visited the site before, and therefore the person testing is not allowed to browse the site before the tasks begin. We will gather data on things like bugs, slow performing pages etc. We will take notes during the whole live testing process.

The live testing consist of seven different tasks, and a section where the test-subject can comment about the experience (what was good, what was bad, what could be improve etc). A detailed list of all the questions can be found in here

Google Form

Design

Questions: What do you think about the design? Do you think the design makes the page easily readable?

The general consensus was that the web site was functional and its minimalistic design helped make the site more accessible. However, some users would have preferred a more colorful design.

Functionality

Questions: Did any functionalities positively stand out? Did anything not work as intended?

Many users found the image and video-gallery to be positively standing out. Some users found the text to be overlapping and the 'Rent Now'-buttons to not be displaying as intended.

Accessibility

Questions: How easy is it to navigate through the site? Were you able to navigate destination on the page on three clicks or less?

As pointed out in the section about the design. Every user reported that they did not need more than three clicks to get to a specific destination.

Performance

Questions: Did anything load too slowly? Is there anything not working as it should?

Some users reported that the videos were loading slowly in the video gallery.

Ideas and Comments

Questions: Is there anything the page is lacking? Do you have any further comments?

Users would like the following functionalities to be added:

  • More colors on the website
  • Rental prices and information on how long you could rent
  • Chatbot
  • Drone sorting filer functionality

Overall, users were very pleased with the site and enjoyed the simplicity and accessibility of the page.
